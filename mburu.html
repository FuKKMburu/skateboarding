History of Skateboarding

Skateboarding began in the 1950s in California and Hawaii when surfers wanted something to do when there were no waves. It started with simple homemade boards and later became a global sport and culture with famous tricks like the ollie.

Famous Skateboarders

Tony Hawk

A legendary skateboarder known for popularizing vert skating and being the first to land the 900 trick. He helped bring skateboarding into the mainstream.

Known as the Godfather of Street Skateboarding, he invented many modern skate tricks like the kickflip and heelflip.

Paul Rodriguez

A pioneer of mega ramp skateboarding, famous for his creativity, big air tricks, and unique skating style.
